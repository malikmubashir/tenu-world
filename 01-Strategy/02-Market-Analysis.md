## Market Size

### France
- **445,000** international students enrolled in 2024-25 (record high, +3% YoY)
- France targets **500,000** international students by 2027
- International students = **14%** of all French higher education students
- Rental tension score hit **4.8** in 2024 (record, up from 3.35 in 2023)
- Average rent: **€723/month** for 42.5m² (2024, up 3.3% vs 2023)
- Deposit capped at **1 month's rent** (unfurnished) / 2 months (furnished)

### UK
- **4.7 million** deposits protected in England & Wales (TDS Briefing 2024-25)
- **£1.3 billion+** in deposits held by mydeposits alone
- **11 million** private renters, **19%** of all UK households
- **23%** of tenants entering disputes submitted no evidence — lost by default
- Average UK deposit: **£1,200–1,800** (5 weeks' rent, capped)
- Formal disputes: **~2.5%** of tenancies annually = ~117,500 cases/year

## Regulatory Tailwinds

### UK — Renters' Rights Act 2025
- Royal Assent: **27 October 2025**
- Key provisions live: **1 May 2026**
- Section 21 "no-fault" evictions abolished
- Lifetime transferable deposit model signalled
- Mandatory PRS Ombudsman (phase 2, ~2028)
- Landlord database + registration (late 2026)
- **Impact on Tenu:** Increases tenant rights complexity → increases demand for guidance tools

### France — loi Alur, loi Élan
- État des lieux mandatory by law (entry + exit)
- Digital signature valid since 2018 (loi Élan)
- Commission Départementale de Conciliation (CDC) — free but requires prepared evidence
- No mandatory national deposit scheme (unlike UK) → less institutional competition, more consumer education needed

## Why International Tenants Are the Priority Segment

| Factor | International Tenant | Domestic Tenant |
|---|---|---|
| Pain intensity | Maximum — unfamiliar language + law | Medium |
| Willingness to pay | High (€15 trivial vs €600–1,800 deposit) | Medium |
| Challenge rate | Very low — language barrier stops them | Higher |
| Distribution | Clustered — university groups, WhatsApp, WeChat | Dispersed |
| CAC | Near zero via community channels | Higher via SEO/ads |
| Word-of-mouth | Very high — tight national communities | Lower |

## Addressable Market Sizing

### Conservative (France + UK move-outs, 5% penetration)
- France move-outs/year: ~1.2M
- UK move-outs/year: ~1.5M
- Total addressable: **2.7M**
- At 5% penetration: **135,000 users**
- At €20 avg transaction: **€2.7M ARR**

### Realistic (10% penetration, blended pricing)
- At 10% penetration: **270,000 users**
- At €25 avg (room-based + dispute mix): **€6.75M ARR**

### International student subsegment only
- France: 445K × 75% renting = 334K × annual move = ~167K users/year
- UK: 700K × 60% private rental = 420K × annual move = ~210K users/year
- Combined: **377K high-pain users** before touching domestic tenant market

## Competitive Intelligence

### Deposit Guard (depositguard.com) — closest competitor
- UK-only, tenant-side, photo evidence tool
- Freemium + small fee to generate report
- No AI risk scoring
- No dispute letter generation
- No multilingual support
- No French market
- **Window:** 12–18 months before they could add multilingual + AI

### DefendMyRent (defendmyrent.com)
- US-only, AI dispute letter generator
- $9.85/letter (currently free)
- No inspection or photo workflow
- No UK or French law
- **Not a direct competitor in EU/UK**

### Compens.ai
- Global AI fairness platform
- Credit-based (€7–100)
- Not deposit-specific
- No photo evidence, no inspection
- **Too broad to be direct competition**
