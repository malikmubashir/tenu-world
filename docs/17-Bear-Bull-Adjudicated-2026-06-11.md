# Tenu.World — Adversarial Strategy Review, Adjudicated Conclusions

**Date:** 2026-06-11 · **Method:** three-agent adversarial review — independent bear case, independent bull case (both grounded in repo + sourced web research), forced cross-examination round, then independent fact-check of 19 contested claims (11 external, 8 internal) with claim-by-claim verdicts. Only material that survived all three rounds appears below. Full source citations are preserved in the round transcripts (session 2026-06-11).

---

## The net ruling

**The bear wins on present facts. The bull wins on the framing of the future — but every bull thesis survives only in weakened form, and one was rejected outright.** Tenu as it stands on 11 June 2026 is a survivable, cheap-to-run experiment with a genuinely unoccupied product wedge, carrying a trust-posture defect that would become a fair accusation of deceit if the company launches on 15 July without closing it.

---

## What survived — the six theses after adjudication

**1. The free-alternative wall (bear) — SUSTAINED, NARROWED.** ANIL/ADIL free templates and counsel, free CDC conciliation, and DemanderJustice's free mise en demeure are all confirmed real. But the wall stands only against the **€20 dispute-letter SKU**. Nothing in the free layer competes with the entry product: pre-emptive, timestamped, room-by-room evidence with AI risk scoring in the tenant's own language, created *before* any dispute exists. Strategic consequence: **the entry scan is the product; the letter is an attach.** Position and market accordingly. (Also corrected: the legaltech comparable is DemanderJustice at €39.90–89.90 current pricing, not the stale €199.90 figure.)

**2. CAC > LTV (bear) — SUSTAINED AS A RISK, NOT A VERDICT.** No CAC data exists in either direction because zero acquisition has been attempted. What is confirmed: one-shot purchase, ~€12.50–30 net per customer, 12–24-month repurchase cycle, and a launch that misses roughly half of the entry-scan seasonal peak (~49% of French moves occur Jun–Sep; 15 July forfeits June and half of July). The F&F cohort and the first SEO/AEO month are the experiment that settles this. **Instrument CAC and conversion from day one or the debate recurs with no new data.**

**3. Trust posture (bear) — SUSTAINED, REPHRASED, AND THE SINGLE DECISIVE FINDING.** Verified in code: `main` sends inspection photos and tenancy details to api.anthropic.com while the published privacy policy promises all data stays in the EU; Art. 17 erasure cascades the database but leaves generated PDFs (address, names, photos) in R2 indefinitely; photo URLs are unsigned and unrevocable; a consent-recording outage ran silently for two weeks in April. "Falsified" was rejected — every gap is candidly documented internally and the DPO process is mid-flight — but **launching 15 July without closing the residency contradiction and the PDF purge converts the bear's weakest rhetorical claim into a true one.** This is the only finding in the review that is binary and date-bound.

**4. Empty quadrant (bull) — SUSTAINED AS HYPOTHESIS.** Confirmed: every French EDL tool is landlord/agency-side (homePad sells consumer credits at €14.99 but is landlord-oriented; Chapps ~€5/inspection B2B); tenant-side + multilingual/RTL + AI-scored + pre-emptive is unoccupied; Morocco and Algeria lead the 443,500 international students (+17%/5yr, state target 500k by 2027). But emptiness is equally consistent with absence of demand — the Bas-Rhin CDC's 81 dossiers/year cuts both ways. **The quadrant is a testable hypothesis, not evidence. The F&F cohort is the test.** Note: homePad's €14.99 anchors the entry tier; the €25–35 tiers must visibly justify the AI delta.

**5. Cheap survival (bull) — SUSTAINED, CORRECTED.** The €47/month burn figure predates the current stack. Honest recurring burn is **~€90–115/month** (MEDICYS, Apple, Workspace, phone line), with €1.5–2.5k one-time legal setup — so **breakeven is ~8–9 paying users/month, not 4.** Still: for a consulting-funded founder taking no salary, this remains the rarest property in consumer legaltech — the company cannot die of burn, only of neglect. LLM price deflation (~10x/yr confirmed) is real but near-irrelevant: COGS is already <8% of net revenue, and deflation equally cheapens any clone.

**6. Compliance-as-moat (bull) — REJECTED.** The only thesis killed outright. The compliance work is currently the *deficit*, not the asset (five overdue p:0 legal blockers); MEDICYS costs €300 and twenty minutes; RGPD conformity is table stakes any funded competitor replicates in weeks. And the one regulatory wall with genuine moat potential — art. 54 périmètre du droit — points the wrong way: the CNB's December 2025 redefinition of "consultation juridique" ("prestation personnalisée… avis fondé sur l'application d'une règle de droit") describes the AI risk scan uncomfortably well. A cost everyone must pay is not a moat.

## Decisions this review forces (entered in TASKS.md)

1. **Close the trust contradiction before 15 July — p:0.** Either (a) merge the Bedrock EU branch before the 8 July freeze, or (b) the cheap path: confirm Anthropic Zero Data Retention (#T132, already open), amend the privacy-policy residency sentence to disclose Anthropic as a ZDR sub-processor accurately, and implement R2 purge (PDFs included) on Art. 17 erasure (#T157 — elevated from p:1 to p:0 by this review).
2. **Correct CLAUDE.md economics** — burn ~€100/mo, breakeven 8–9 users/month. Decisions made on the 4-user figure are decisions made on fiction.
3. **Correct the store strategy language.** "Reader App model" is factually shaky (reader apps = media content). The accurate EU mechanism is the DMA external-purchase-link regime: link-outs are permitted on the French storefront at a 0–12% fee depending on in-app promotion design — materially better than the bear's "rejection or 15–30%" and different from the bull's "Apple cannot touch us."
4. **Add art. 54 to the avocat brief.** The formal sign-off (#T111) must explicitly opine on whether the AI risk scan falls inside the CNB's proposed "consultation juridique" definition, and on the document-generation safe harbour (CA Paris 2018, DemanderJustice).
5. **Lead the launch window with exit scans.** The 15 July date misses half the entry-scan peak but lands at the start of exit season (leases ending Jul–Oct). The €25 exit_only SKU is the seasonal spearhead; entry scans are the September re-rental harvest.
6. **Finish or stop claiming the 10 languages.** Five of ten locales map to English dictionaries. Either complete hi/ja/es/pt/ko or scope the marketing claim to the five real ones until then.
7. **Instrument from user one:** acquisition source, conversion step, CAC per channel, scan→letter attach rate, 14-day outcome. Every unresolved question in this review dies or survives on that data.

## The one-paragraph verdict

Tenu is not the lifestyle-business-pretending-to-be-a-venture the bear describes, nor the moated compounder the bull describes. It is a **cheap, well-built option on an unproven hypothesis** — that tenants who cannot discover France's free remedies will pay €15–35 for pre-emptive evidence in their own language. The economics make the option nearly free to hold; the July cohort prices it. The only way to lose unrecoverably this summer is to launch with the trust posture contradicting the privacy policy — fix that, run the experiment honestly, and let the data, not the debate, decide the Series-anything question.
