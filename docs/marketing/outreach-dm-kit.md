## 1. Stance

We do not spray. We send 20 to 30 targeted DMs per week, each individually written on top of one of these templates. Every message acknowledges that we are the party interrupting someone's feed, and offers something they can accept or decline in one read.

Three rules, non-negotiable:

1. Never claim the tool guarantees deposit recovery. It does not.
2. Never promise legal advice. Tenu is a template generator.
3. Never use the word "student" where the recipient is a tenant, not a student. Precision costs nothing.

Response rate target: 12% reply, 4% convert to trial. Below 8% reply, rework the template. Above 20% reply, scale.

## 4. Voice and style rules

- No em dashes.
- No exclamation marks.
- No "In today's world" opener.
- No "Hope this finds you well."
- No emoji except in segment S5 where warmth requires it and then no more than one per message.
- First person singular, not the royal we, until a formal business context (Template D) justifies it.
- Length target: 80 to 150 words. Under 80 reads abrupt, over 150 gets skipped.
- One explicit statement per message that Tenu is not legal advice. This protects us, and it builds trust by removing the obvious overclaim.

## 6. Reply handling playbook

### "How does it work?"
```
You upload 3-5 photos per room via the phone. The tool detects
visible wear and applies the vetuste grid (8 years for paint,
10 years for floors, etc.). You get a PDF report listing what
your landlord can reasonably deduct. If you want, you add a
dispute letter for 25 EUR that you can send recorded delivery.

You keep the originals, we never train AI on your photos.

Want a free trial code?
```

### "Isn't this just a lawyer's job?"
```
A lawyer does the legal strategy. The tool does the boring
drafting part — measuring wear, writing the LRAR in the
right format. Most deposit disputes don't need a lawyer, they
need a firm letter sent by registered mail within the legal
deadline. That's what we produce.
```

### "What's your data policy?"
```
Photos stored encrypted on EU servers (Cloudflare R2 Frankfurt),
deleted after 14 months, never used to train AI. Full policy:
tenu.world/en/privacy. We're a French SAS, CNIL rules apply.
```

### "Is this actually legal in France?"
```
Yes — it's a document template, not legal representation. French
law (loi 71-1130) only restricts who can give legal advice and
act in court. Templates and form-filling are not reserved. Same
logic as Doctolib for medical appointments: we don't practise,
we facilitate.
```

### "Can I see an example report?"
```
Link: tenu.world/en/sample (anonymised, 3 rooms, 2 wear deductions).
```

### Silence after DM
After 72 hours: one short follow-up, never more.
```
Hi [name], following up in case my message got buried. No worry
if this isn't a fit. If it is, I'm around this week.
```

Then stop. Three bumps is harassment.

## 8. Legal and compliance guardrails

- Reddit and FB spam policies: no identical message across multiple communities within 24h. Use semantic variants.
- LinkedIn: InMail limit is 25 per month free. Use personal DMs with first-degree connections only.
- WhatsApp: no bulk lists. Personal send only. Bulk list is a GDPR violation (no lawful basis).
- Email (Template D): Tenu is a SAS operating a commercial service, so email outreach to professional addresses is allowed under RGPD art. 6.1.f (legitimate interest) with clear opt-out in every message.
- Every email includes: sender identity (Global Apex.Net SAS, RCS Versailles 941 666 067, siege 4 Boulevard du Chateau 78280 Guyancourt), clear unsubscribe path, no tracking pixel in first contact.

## 9. Critique of this kit (self-check)

Weaknesses that Dr Mubashir should decide about:

1. **Templates A, B, C are EN only.** No FR variant yet. For a French market that is a gap. Fix before sending: produce French mirror of at least Template A by 2026-04-22.
2. **No video intro.** A 45-second Loom from Dr Mubashir would raise reply rates on Template D materially — adds ~2 hours to produce but plausibly doubles conversion.
3. **No testimonials.** We are pre-launch, there are none. Acceptable at this stage; revisit after 10 paying users.
4. **University email is optimistic.** International offices receive many pitches. Reply rate under 5% is the realistic baseline. Strategy: go through 1-2 warm intros from Dr Mubashir's professional Global Apex network before generic send.
5. **No Urdu or Arabic template yet.** Warm community outreach (segment S5) would benefit. Defer until Dr Mubashir has first-contact confirmed with a community lead, then write the message with that person.
