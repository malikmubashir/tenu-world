**Subject:** Fixed-fee legal review — online tenant deposit assistance service (TDS / DPS / MyDeposits)

Dear [Name],

I'm writing on behalf of Global Apex NET SAS, a French-incorporated company launching a consumer-facing online service called **Tenu** (tenu.world). We help tenants — primarily international students and expats — build evidence around their rental property and, where appropriate, generate a deposit-dispute letter to the landlord. We're preparing the UK leg of the product and need a solicitor's sign-off on the UK template before we go live.

**What Tenu does, in two sentences.** Tenants take time-stamped photos of their property at move-in and move-out, the service scores deduction risk per room, and — if the landlord makes deductions the tenant disputes — generates a letter formatted for the relevant scheme: TDS, DPS or MyDeposits. The output is a draft for the tenant to review, edit and send. Tenu is not a regulated legal service and we do not act on the tenant's behalf; the letter is theirs.

**Audience.** UK private-rental tenants (assured shorthold tenancies). Direct-to-consumer. Pay-per-use, GBP 15-35 per scan plus a GBP 20 dispute-letter add-on. No subscription.

**What I'm asking you to review.** Before we ship the UK product, I need a solicitor with current housing-law practice to review:

1. **Dispute-letter template** — the structure of the generated letter against TDS, DPS and MyDeposits dispute-resolution requirements (deduction calculation, evidence references, scheme procedural deadlines, statutory rights cited).
2. **UK terms of service + privacy policy** at tenu.world/legal — Consumer Rights Act 2015 alignment, distance-selling cancellation rights and our reliance on the "fully performed digital service" exception, ICO / UK GDPR position on photo handling and cross-border data flows (data is stored in the EU).
3. **Static rights-explainer copy** — ten plain-English statements we display to UK tenants (deposit protection rules, the 30-day registration window, the 5-week cap under the Tenant Fees Act 2019, the deposit return timeline, the right to escalate via the scheme adjudication, etc.). Not AI-generated; pre-written and version-pinned.
4. **AI disclosure language** — we use Anthropic Claude server-side for letter drafting and risk scoring. I want to make sure our user-facing wording is honest and defensible, and that we're not crossing into reserved-activity territory under the Legal Services Act 2007.

**Format.**
- One read-through of the package (terms, privacy, DPA, dispute-letter template, ten facts) — roughly **30-40 pages**.
- A written response: items compliant, items to fix (ranked by severity), recommended changes.
- A **30-45 minute call** to close out the review.
- Turnaround target: **by 7 May 2026**.

**Fee.** Fixed fee in the range of **GBP 500 to GBP 1,500**, depending on the scope you confirm. Happy to receive your quote; if you assess the work above this band, please tell me before we proceed and we'll discuss.

**Materials I'll send on request.** UK terms of service v1.0, privacy policy v1.0 (UK GDPR section), DPA v1.0, dispute-letter template (EN, TDS / DPS / MyDeposits variants), ten-facts UK table, one-page Tenu summary. I'll forward the bundle as soon as you confirm interest.
